**Stay Safe Online Checklist**



1.The "Golden Rule" of UPI: Never enter your UPI PIN to receive money. A PIN is only required to send or deduct money from your account.



2.Verify the Requester: Before clicking on any "Collect Request" in apps like GPay or PhonePe, double-check the name and UPI ID of the person asking for money.



3.Enable Two-Factor Authentication (2FA): Activate 2FA on your Gmail, LinkedIn, and social media accounts to add an extra layer of security beyond just a password.



4.Use Strong, Unique Passwords: Avoid using easily guessable passwords like your name or birthdate. Use a mix of symbols, numbers, and capital letters.



5.Beware of Phishing Links: Do not click on links in SMS or emails that claim your bank account is "blocked" or that you have won a "mega prize."



6.Secure Your Hardware: Set a strong biometric lock (fingerprint or face ID) on your smartphone to prevent unauthorized access if your phone is lost or stolen.



7.Avoid Public Wi-Fi for Banking: Never log into your bank account or shopping apps while connected to free public Wi-Fi in cafes or airports.



8.Regularly Update Apps: Keep your banking and payment apps updated to the latest version to ensure you have the most recent security patches.



**Official Reporting Channels :**



If you or someone you know falls victim to a cybercrime, immediately use the following official Government of India channels:



National Cyber Crime Reporting Portal: www.cybercrime.gov.in



National Cyber Crime Helpline Number: 1930 (Available 24/7)
